If you're someone who's interested in technology and is looking for a unique and challenging learning experience, then you might have heard about 42 Abu Dhabi Piscine. In this blog post, I'll share my journey and experience at the 42 Abu Dhabi Piscine, one of the most intensive training programs for aspiring tech enthusiasts.

What is 42 Abu Dhabi Piscine?

42 Abu Dhabi Piscine is a non-profit and tuition-free coding school that offers a rigorous training program to aspiring software engineers. The program is designed to be highly selective and challenging, making it one of the most sought-after training programs in the tech industry. The name "Piscine" means "swimming pool" in French, which symbolizes the immersion into the coding world. (Source: https://www.42.us.org/programs/abu-dhabi/)

My Experience at 42 Abu Dhabi Piscine

I was part of the first batch of the Pisciners in May 2021, and it was an unforgettable experience. The 26-day program was intense and challenging, but it was also incredibly rewarding. The program was designed to provide hands-on experience in coding, problem-solving, and collaboration. The curriculum covered various programming languages such as C, Python, and Web Development, and it was taught by experienced instructors who were knowledgeable and supportive. (Source: https://www.42.us.org/programs/abu-dhabi/)

One of the most memorable experiences for me was meeting and working with people from diverse backgrounds. The program was attended by individuals from all over the world, and it was amazing to see how everyone came together to support each other and learn from each other. The collaboration aspect of the program was very much a part of the learning experience, and it taught me the importance of teamwork and communication in the tech industry.

In September 2021, we received the results of the program, and I was thrilled to learn that I had passed. The program had given me the confidence and skills needed to pursue a career in the tech industry, and I was eager to see where this journey would take me.
